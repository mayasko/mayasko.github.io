👋 About

He is a researcher with the Power Electronics Team of the ELECTA research Group at KU Leuven and EnergyVille research center (Belgium). He was a visiting researcher at the University of Nottingham in the PEMC group (UK). He conducted his PhD research on charging efficiency for electric vehicles. He developed several solutions for improving efficiency for charging infrastructure. Additionally, he contributed to advancing understanding and practical implementation for smart charging methods. He was teaching associate and responsible of courses such as power electronics, electrical energy and drives, etc. He (co)supervised more than six master thesis projects and gave some seminars. He was also a guest lecturer in different courses of master programs in two universities such as electric vehicles, electromobility etc.

He was also work package responsible in industrial projects such as HUME, REFLEX, were optimal concepts for EV charging efficiency were investigated. His main research interests are in electrical energy systems with particular emphasis on power converters, high power charging systems, energy efficiency, etc. He is actively involved in the Transportation Electrification Community, where he played a role of ITEC track chair and regular reviewer of IEEE transaction journals.

🔬 Research

▶EV Charging Efficiency
Measurement, modeling, and analysis of EV charging losses under different operating conditions.
▶Smart Charging Control
Battery-aware and efficiency-aware charging control using ML, Neural networks and attention-based models and optimization-based methods.
▶Power Electronics
Converter topologies, control, and loss reduction for EV chargers and energy conversion systems.
